1. What is the second definition of a homeless individual or family?
2. What are the three conditions that must be met for an individual or family to be considered homeless under the second definition?
3. How long do individuals or families have to lose their residence before they are considered homeless under the second definition?
1. The second definition of a homeless individual or family is one who will imminently lose their primary nighttime residence.
2. The three conditions that must be met for an individual or family to be considered homeless under the second definition are that residence will be lost within 14 days of the date of application for homeless assistance, no subsequent residence has been identified, and the individual or family lacks the resources or support networks needed to obtain other permanent housing.
3. Individuals or families have 14 days to lose their residence before they are considered homeless under the second definition.
