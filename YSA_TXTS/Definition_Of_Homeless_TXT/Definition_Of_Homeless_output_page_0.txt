1. What are the eligibility requirements for the Tiny House Empowerment Village transitional housing program?
2. What are the four categories of individuals who are considered "literally" homeless?
3. How do you become a resident of the Tiny House Empowerment Village transitional housing program?
1. The eligibility requirements for the Tiny House Empowerment Village transitional housing program are that you are between the ages of 18-23, identify either Oakland or Berkeley as your home city, and are currently "literally" homeless by HomeStretch standards.
2. The four categories of individuals who are considered "literally" homeless are individuals or families who lack a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence, individuals or families who will imminently lose their primary nighttime residence, unaccompanied youth under 25 years of age, or families with Category 3 children and youth, who do not otherwise qualify as homeless.
3. To become a resident of the Tiny House Empowerment Village transitional housing program, you must meet the eligibility requirements and complete an application.
